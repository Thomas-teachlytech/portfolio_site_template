Key Findings

Finding 1 — Data Volume Is Not the Primary Failure Driver

For Chained and Format rules, every model held its passing tier across all payload sizes. Haiku passed Format T1-T2 with 100% soft scores from Medium through XL. Opus passed Chained T1-T5 and Format T1-T5 without a single failure across all payload sizes. The hypothesis that more tabular data degrades rule adherence is largely not supported for structured and conditional rule types at this scale.

Finding 2 — Scope Failures Are Semantic, Not Volumetric

Opus failed Scope T4 across all payload sizes but passed T5 at Large and XL after failing at Medium. The inconsistency rules out data volume as the cause. The violated rules were consistently the inference-restraint rules: do not derive figures, do not describe trends beyond the dataset. More data gives the model more to infer from, but failures appear stochastic rather than threshold-driven. Scope is a hard rule type for Opus at T4+ regardless of payload size.

Finding 3 — Sonnet Shows a Genuine Volume Effect on Format Bookend Rules

Sonnet passed Format T3 cleanly at Medium but failed at Large and XL — always on the same rule: the exact closing phrase "Data sourced from your portfolio dataset." At XL payload, Format T2 failed with a 0% soft score — Sonnet dropped both opening and closing bookend phrases entirely. As the response grows longer to cover more tickers and periods, Sonnet loses track of structural bookend requirements. This is the clearest data volume effect in Round 3.

Finding 4 — Haiku Is Stable Within Its Safe Zone

Haiku passed Format T1 and T2 with 100% soft scores across all payload sizes. Within the tier range where it was previously passing, Haiku showed no sensitivity to data volume. This confirms that within its safe zone, volume is not an additional risk factor — the ceiling remains instruction complexity from Round 2, not context size.

Finding 5 — Sonnet Chained T3 Failure at Medium Did Not Persist

Sonnet failed Chained T3 at Medium (67% soft score) but passed the same tier at Large and XL. This is the opposite of a volume effect — likely a one-off edge case in the Medium dataset where a ticker's data fell near a conditional threshold. It should not be treated as a finding about data volume and may reflect judge sensitivity as much as model behaviour.
