Key findings

Scope is the platform's primary failure point

All three models failed Scope from T2 onward. Combined pass rate 6/21 (29%). Weight 0.71 — highest in the suite. Scope alone accounts for most of the gap between raw and weighted scores for Sonnet and Haiku.

Weighted scores reveal a larger capability gap than raw scores show

Raw scores: Opus 92% · Sonnet 84% · Haiku 71% (21-point spread). Weighted scores: Opus 77% · Sonnet 52% · Haiku 36% (41-point spread). Weighting amplifies failures on hard categories and shows the real separation between model tiers.

Situation separates model tiers sharply

Opus 7/7. Sonnet 5/7. Haiku 1/7. Haiku failed at T1 — before any rule stacking. Conditional detection (if X then Y) appears to require significantly more capacity than Haiku reliably has at any rule count.

Chained logic degrades all models — tier of first failure tracks model size

Opus first failed at T6. Sonnet at T5. Haiku at T4. Two-condition logic is the clearest discriminator of model capacity within this platform. First failure tier maps directly to model tier.

Zero-weight categories cannot differentiate models

Content, Style, Priority: perfect scores across all three models at all seven tiers. These categories should be deprioritised in cross-category testing — they contribute no signal about model capability.
